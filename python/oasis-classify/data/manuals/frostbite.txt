Category: Frostbite

STEPS:
1. Move the person to a warm environment immediately.
2. Remove wet gloves, socks, or footwear from frostbitten areas gently.
3. Do not rewarm if there is any chance the area will refreeze — refreezing causes worse damage.
4. Rewarm frozen tissue by immersing in warm water (37-40 degrees C, comfortable to a non-injured elbow) for 20-30 minutes.
5. The tissue will become red and painful as it warms — this is normal.
6. Cover the rewarmed area with clean dry dressings; place padding between frostbitten fingers or toes.
7. Give pain relief if available — rewarming is very painful.
8. Seek medical care for all but the most superficial frostbite.

NEVER DO:
- Do NOT rub or massage frostbitten tissue — the ice crystals inside cause further damage.
- Do NOT use fire, stove heat, or a heating pad — uneven heat causes burns to numb tissue.
- Do NOT walk on frostbitten feet unless absolutely necessary for survival.
- Do NOT pop or drain blisters that form.
