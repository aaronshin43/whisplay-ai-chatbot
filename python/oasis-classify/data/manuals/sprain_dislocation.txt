Category: Sprain / Dislocation

STEPS:
1. Stop the activity and rest the injured joint immediately.
2. Apply ICE wrapped in a cloth to the joint for 20 minutes to reduce swelling — do not apply ice directly to skin.
3. Apply a compression bandage firmly but not too tightly around the joint.
4. Elevate the injured limb above heart level to reduce swelling.
5. For a suspected dislocation, immobilize the joint in the position found — do not attempt to reduce it.
6. If unable to bear weight, the joint appears deformed, or there is significant swelling, seek medical evaluation.
7. Recheck the bandage frequently to ensure circulation is not impaired.
8. Over the following days: rest, ice 20 min several times daily, compress, elevate (RICE).

NEVER DO:
- Do NOT attempt to pop a dislocated joint back in without medical training — nerve and vessel damage can result.
- Do NOT apply heat in the first 24-48 hours — it increases swelling.
- Do NOT apply ice directly to bare skin — wrap it first.
- Do NOT bear weight on a dislocated or severely injured joint.
