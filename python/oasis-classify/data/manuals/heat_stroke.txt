Category: Heat Stroke / Heat Exhaustion

STEPS:
1. Call emergency services (911) for heat stroke (hot dry skin, confusion, no sweating, very high body temperature).
2. Move the person immediately to a cool shaded or air-conditioned area.
3. Remove excess clothing.
4. Cool the person aggressively: apply ice packs or cold wet cloths to neck, armpits, and groin.
5. Fan the person while misting with cool water.
6. If fully conscious and able to swallow, give cool water to drink.
7. For heat exhaustion (heavy sweating, dizziness, weakness but alert): move to cool area, give fluids, rest.
8. Monitor closely — heat exhaustion can progress to heat stroke.

NEVER DO:
- Do NOT give alcohol or caffeine.
- Do NOT give fluids to an unconscious person or one who cannot swallow.
- Do NOT delay cooling while waiting for emergency services — cooling must start immediately.
- Do NOT cover the person with a heavy blanket — this traps heat.
