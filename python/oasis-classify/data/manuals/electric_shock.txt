Category: Electric Shock / Electrocution

STEPS:
1. Do NOT touch the person until the power source is confirmed off — you will also be electrocuted.
2. Call emergency services (911) immediately.
3. Turn off the power at the main switch or circuit breaker before approaching.
4. If you cannot cut power, use a dry non-conductive object (wooden board, rubber mat) to push the source away.
5. Once the person is safe to approach, check for responsiveness and breathing.
6. If not breathing, begin CPR immediately — cardiac arrest is common after electric shock.
7. Cover any burns with a clean dry dressing — do not apply creams or ice.
8. Keep the person still and warm until emergency services arrive.

NEVER DO:
- Do NOT touch the person while they are still in contact with the electrical source.
- Do NOT use wet or metal objects to move a live electrical source.
- Do NOT assume low voltage is safe — household current can cause cardiac arrest.
- Do NOT apply ice or ointments to electrical burns.
