Category: Altitude Sickness

STEPS:
1. Take all altitude sickness symptoms seriously — they can progress to life-threatening HACE or HAPE.
2. Stop ascending and rest at the current altitude if symptoms are mild (headache, nausea, fatigue).
3. Descend immediately if symptoms are moderate to severe: confusion, loss of coordination, severe headache, breathlessness at rest.
4. Descent by at least 500-1000 meters is the most effective treatment.
5. Administer supplemental oxygen if available — breathe at 2-4 liters per minute.
6. Dexamethasone 8 mg initial dose can be given for severe AMS or HACE if available.
7. Nifedipine 30 mg extended-release is first-line for HAPE if available.
8. Never leave a symptomatic person alone at altitude.

NEVER DO:
- Do NOT continue ascending while experiencing any altitude sickness symptoms.
- Do NOT take sleeping pills — they suppress breathing and worsen hypoxia.
- Do NOT assume symptoms will pass on their own above 3000 meters without descending.
- Do NOT delay descent while waiting for medication to work if condition is worsening.
