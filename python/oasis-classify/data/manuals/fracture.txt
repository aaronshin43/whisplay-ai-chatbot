Category: Fracture / Broken Bone

STEPS:
1. Call emergency services (911/999/112) if the fracture is open, involves the spine, pelvis, or femur, or if shock is present.
2. Do not attempt to straighten or realign the bone.
3. Immobilize the limb as found: use an improvised splint (rigid item padded with cloth) on both sides of the fracture.
4. Secure the splint above and below the fracture site with bandages or strips of cloth.
5. Check circulation beyond the splint: pulse, sensation, and color at fingers or toes.
6. If the fracture is open, cover loosely with a clean dressing — do not push bone back in.
7. Apply ice wrapped in cloth to reduce swelling — do not apply directly to skin.
8. Elevate the limb gently if no spinal injury is suspected.

NEVER DO:
- Do NOT attempt to push a protruding bone back into the skin.
- Do NOT straighten or reposition the limb.
- Do NOT apply a splint too tightly — recheck circulation every 10 minutes.
- Do NOT give food or drink if surgery may be needed.
