Category: Lost in Wilderness

STEPS:
1. STOP: Stop moving immediately. Panic causes poor decisions and burns energy.
2. Think: Recall your last known position, the route taken, and any landmarks.
3. Observe: Note the sun position, terrain features, water sources, and your supplies.
4. Plan: Decide whether to stay put or move — staying put is almost always the correct choice.
5. Stay put unless in immediate danger — rescuers search along known trails first.
6. Build shelter immediately if night or rain is approaching — exposure kills faster than dehydration or hunger.
7. Signal for rescue in groups of three: three whistle blasts, three fires in a triangle, three flashes with a mirror.
8. If you must move: follow water downstream toward civilization; mark your trail.
9. Conserve phone battery for a single emergency call or GPS position share.

NEVER DO:
- Do NOT keep moving when lost hoping to find your way — you will cover more ground to search.
- Do NOT ignore shelter in favor of food or water — hypothermia is the greatest immediate threat.
- Do NOT signal alone with a single fire — three signals is the universal distress code.
