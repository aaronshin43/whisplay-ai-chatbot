Category: Wild Animal Attack

STEPS:
1. Do NOT run — running triggers the predator's chase response.
2. Make yourself large: stand tall, raise your arms, open your jacket wide, and group with others.
3. Maintain direct eye contact and face the animal at all times.
4. Speak loudly and firmly; shout and make noise to appear threatening.
5. If a mountain lion attacks: fight back immediately — strike the nose, eyes, and throat with fists, sticks, or rocks. Do NOT play dead.
6. If wolves or coyotes attack: fight back with any available object and protect your neck and face.
7. Back away slowly if the animal pauses — create distance without turning away.
8. After the attack: control bleeding and call 911 for medical help.

NEVER DO:
- Do NOT run from a mountain lion, wolf, or coyote — it confirms you as prey.
- Do NOT turn your back on a predator — always keep it in your line of sight.
- Do NOT play dead with a mountain lion — fight back aggressively.
- Do NOT crouch down or make yourself smaller — it signals submission.
