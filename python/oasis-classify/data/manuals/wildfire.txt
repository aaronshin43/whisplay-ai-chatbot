Category: Wildfire Escape

STEPS:
1. Check wind direction immediately — fire travels fastest uphill and in the direction the wind blows.
2. Move perpendicular to the fire's path to reach an escape route; do not run parallel to it.
3. Never run uphill into or toward the fire — fire travels uphill much faster than a person can run.
4. Protect your airway: wet a cloth and hold it over your nose and mouth to filter smoke.
5. Stay low where air is cooler and smoke is thinner if caught in heavy smoke.
6. If escape is cut off: find a ditch or cleared area with minimal vegetation and lie face-down with hands protecting your face.
7. Clear dry vegetation around your position if time allows.
8. Signal rescuers with a mirror or bright cloth and call 911 as soon as you have signal.

NEVER DO:
- Do NOT run uphill toward a wildfire — fire moves uphill faster than you can run.
- Do NOT shelter in a building that could collapse or trap you in the fire's path.
- Do NOT ignore wind shifts — fire direction can change and cut off escape routes suddenly.
