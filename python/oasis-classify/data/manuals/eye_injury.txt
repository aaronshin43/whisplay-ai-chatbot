Category: Eye Injury

STEPS:
1. Call emergency services or seek immediate medical care.
2. For chemical splash: flush the eye immediately with large amounts of clean water for at least 20 minutes continuously.
3. Hold the eye open under gently flowing water — tilt the head so the affected eye is lower to prevent contamination of the other eye.
4. For a foreign body (grit, dust): do not rub — try blinking rapidly or flushing with clean water.
5. If a foreign body is embedded or the eye is impaled, do not touch it — cover gently with a clean cup or cone.
6. For a wound to the eye, cover both eyes lightly with a clean dry dressing — covering both reduces movement.
7. Keep the person calm and prevent rubbing.
8. For a chemical burn, identify the substance and report it to medical staff.

NEVER DO:
- Do NOT rub the eye when a foreign body is present.
- Do NOT attempt to remove an embedded or impaled object from the eye.
- Do NOT apply pressure to the eye itself.
- Do NOT use dry cotton wool directly on the eye surface.
