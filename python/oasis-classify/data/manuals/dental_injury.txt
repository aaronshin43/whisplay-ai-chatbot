Category: Dental Injury

STEPS:
1. Find the knocked-out tooth and handle it by the crown only — do not touch the root.
2. If dirty, rinse gently with milk or saline — do not scrub or use plain water.
3. Try to reinsert the tooth into the socket immediately if the person is conscious — push gently, bite down on cloth.
4. If unable to reinsert, store in milk, inside the person's cheek, or in saline.
5. See a dentist or emergency room within 30-60 minutes — survival drops rapidly after 1 hour.
6. For mouth bleeding: apply direct pressure with gauze or cloth — have them bite down.
7. For jaw injury: do not force the mouth open or closed — immobilize gently and seek emergency care.
8. Do not attempt to remove teeth or bone fragments from wounds.

NEVER DO:
- Do NOT scrub the tooth root or let it dry out.
- Do NOT store the tooth in plain tap water for extended periods — it damages root cells.
- Do NOT apply aspirin directly on a toothache — it can burn gum tissue.
- Do NOT force the jaw closed if fracture is suspected.
