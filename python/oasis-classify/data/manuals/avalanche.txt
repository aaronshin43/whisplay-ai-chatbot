Category: Avalanche Survival

STEPS:
1. If caught in an avalanche: discard poles and skis immediately to reduce drag.
2. Use a swimming motion to fight toward the surface and the side of the flow.
3. As the snow slows: cover your face with your arms and create an air pocket in front of your nose and mouth before the snow sets hard.
4. Spit once the snow stops — saliva falls toward down; this tells you which direction to dig.
5. Push one arm toward the surface to create a marker point for rescuers.
6. Stay calm and breathe slowly to conserve oxygen in the air pocket.
7. Do not shout unless rescuers are directly above — shouting wastes oxygen; snow muffles sound.
8. If rescuing someone: mark the last seen point, dig from below the burial site, and clear the airway first.

NEVER DO:
- Do NOT panic and thrash after the snow stops — this compresses your air space rapidly.
- Do NOT wait to create an air pocket — snow sets concrete-hard within seconds.
- Do NOT dig straight down toward a buried person — approach from the side and below.
