Category: Panic Attack

STEPS:
1. Stay calm and speak in a slow, reassuring voice — your calm presence is the most important tool.
2. Remind them that what they feel is very uncomfortable but not dangerous and will pass.
3. Guide box breathing: inhale 4 counts, hold 4, exhale 4, hold 4. Repeat until breathing slows.
4. If breathing is too difficult, use 5-4-3-2-1 grounding: name 5 things you see, 4 you touch, 3 you hear, 2 you smell, 1 you taste.
5. Encourage them to sit or lie down. Loosen any tight clothing.
6. Stay with them until symptoms subside, usually within 10 to 20 minutes.
7. If chest pain, left arm pain, or symptoms do not resolve, call 911 — these may indicate a cardiac event.

NEVER DO:
- Do NOT tell them to calm down or just breathe without guiding them through it.
- Do NOT leave them alone during the episode.
- Do NOT assume it is a heart attack without cardiac indicators, but do NOT dismiss cardiac symptoms.
- Do NOT restrain or crowd the person.
