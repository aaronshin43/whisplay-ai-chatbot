Category: Opioid Overdose

STEPS:
1. Call 911 immediately.
2. Check for signs: unresponsive, slow or stopped breathing, blue lips, pinpoint pupils.
3. If naloxone (Narcan) is available, administer one dose — nasal spray or injection per label instructions.
4. Perform rescue breathing while waiting for naloxone to take effect — 1 breath every 5 seconds.
5. If no response after 2-3 minutes, administer a second dose of naloxone.
6. Place in recovery position if breathing returns.
7. If breathing stops completely, begin full CPR.
8. Stay with the person — naloxone wears off in 30-90 minutes and overdose can return.

NEVER DO:
- Do NOT leave the person alone after administering naloxone.
- Do NOT put the person in a cold shower or give coffee — these do not reverse overdose.
- Do NOT assume one dose is enough — fentanyl overdoses may need multiple doses.
- Do NOT give naloxone to a conscious person without overdose signs.
