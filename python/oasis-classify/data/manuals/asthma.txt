Category: Asthma Attack

STEPS:
1. Keep the person calm and help them sit upright — leaning slightly forward with hands on knees opens the airways.
2. If a rescue inhaler (blue, short-acting) is available, help them use it: shake, place in mouth, breathe in as they press.
3. Use a spacer with the inhaler if available for better delivery.
4. Give 1 puff every minute up to 10 puffs while waiting for help.
5. Call 911 if no improvement after 10 puffs, no inhaler available, or breathing worsens.
6. Do not lay the person flat — this makes breathing harder.
7. If unconscious, lay them down and check for breathing.
8. Begin CPR if not breathing.

NEVER DO:
- Do NOT lay the person flat during an asthma attack.
- Do NOT substitute a long-acting brown or purple inhaler for the rescue blue inhaler.
- Do NOT leave the person alone during an attack.
- Do NOT allow the person to continue exerting themselves.
