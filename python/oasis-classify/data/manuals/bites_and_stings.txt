Category: Bites and Stings

STEPS:
1. Call emergency services (911/999/112) for any sign of anaphylaxis, venomous snake bite, or multiple stings.
2. For snake bite: keep calm and still, immobilize the bitten limb at or below heart level, remove nearby jewelry.
3. Identify the snake if safely possible without approaching it.
4. For bee or wasp sting: scrape stinger sideways with a card, wash with soap and water, apply ice.
5. For tick bite: grasp tick with fine tweezers as close to skin as possible and pull straight out steadily.
6. For dog or animal bite: wash thoroughly with soap and water for at least 5 minutes, apply antiseptic.
7. Seek medical care for all animal bites and any venomous bite or sting.
8. Monitor for allergic reaction for at least 30 minutes after any sting.

NEVER DO:
- Do NOT cut and suck venom from a snake bite — ineffective and dangerous.
- Do NOT apply a tourniquet for a snake bite.
- Do NOT crush or squeeze a tick body while removing it.
- Do NOT apply heat or fire to remove a tick.
