Category: Abdominal Injury

STEPS:
1. Call 911 immediately.
2. Lay the person on their back with knees slightly bent to relax abdominal muscles.
3. Do not attempt to push any protruding organs back inside the body.
4. If organs are protruding (evisceration), cover them with a clean cloth moistened with sterile or clean water.
5. Cover the moist dressing loosely with plastic wrap or a clean dry dressing to retain moisture and warmth.
6. Do not apply direct pressure over protruding organs.
7. Keep the person warm to prevent shock.
8. Monitor for signs of internal bleeding: increasing pain, rigid abdomen, pale clammy skin, rapid pulse.

NEVER DO:
- Do NOT push protruding organs back inside.
- Do NOT give food, drink, or oral medication — surgery may be required.
- Do NOT apply ice directly to an abdominal wound.
- Do NOT remove an impaled object from the abdomen.
