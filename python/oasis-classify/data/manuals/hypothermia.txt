Category: Hypothermia

STEPS:
1. Call 911 if the person is severely hypothermic (rigid, unresponsive, or not breathing).
2. Move the person to a warm, dry location out of wind and cold immediately.
3. Remove any wet clothing carefully and replace with dry insulating layers.
4. Cover the person including the head with blankets to retain heat.
5. Apply chemical heat packs or warm water bottles wrapped in cloth to the neck, armpits, and groin — never directly on skin.
6. Give warm sweet drinks if the person is fully conscious and able to swallow.
7. If the person is unresponsive and not breathing, begin CPR — a cold heart can still be resuscitated.
8. Seek hospital care for all but mild hypothermia — core temperature must be measured medically.

NEVER DO:
- Do NOT rub the skin vigorously — cold blood from the extremities can trigger cardiac arrest.
- Do NOT give alcohol — it causes further heat loss.
- Do NOT apply direct heat sources (hot water, fire) — peripheral warming before core rewarming is dangerous.
- Do NOT assume a cold person is dead without medical evaluation.
