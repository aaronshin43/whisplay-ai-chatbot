Category: Diabetic Emergency

STEPS:
1. Determine if the person is conscious and able to swallow.
2. If conscious and able to swallow, assume hypoglycemia (low blood sugar) and give sugar: 15-20 g fast-acting sugar such as glucose tablets, juice, or sugary drink.
3. Have the person sit down and rest.
4. Repeat the sugary drink if no improvement in 15 minutes.
5. After sugar is taken and the person improves, give a complex carbohydrate snack (crackers, bread) to maintain blood sugar.
6. Call 911 if the person is unconscious, cannot swallow, or does not improve.
7. If unconscious, place in the recovery position and call 911 — do not give anything by mouth.
8. Monitor breathing and consciousness until help arrives.

NEVER DO:
- Do NOT give food or drink to an unconscious person.
- Do NOT give insulin without a blood glucose reading.
- Do NOT assume hyperglycemia without testing — when in doubt treat for low blood sugar.
- Do NOT leave the person alone until they are fully recovered.
