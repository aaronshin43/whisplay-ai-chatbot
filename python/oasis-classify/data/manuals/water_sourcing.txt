Category: Water Sourcing / Dehydration

STEPS:
1. Recognize dehydration: dark yellow urine, dizziness, dry mouth, headache, and rapid heart rate.
2. Find water: moving water (streams, rivers) is safer than still water (ponds, puddles).
3. Purify all wild water before drinking — never assume any source is safe.
4. To boil: bring to a rolling boil for 1 minute; at altitudes above 2000 meters, boil for 3 minutes.
5. Chemical treatment: use iodine or chlorine tablets per instructions — wait 30 minutes before drinking.
6. Drink before you feel thirsty — thirst is a late indicator of dehydration.
7. For severe dehydration: give fluids slowly and steadily. Call 911 if the person is confused or unconscious.

NEVER DO:
- Do NOT drink seawater — it accelerates dehydration and damages organs.
- Do NOT drink urine — it concentrates waste and worsens dehydration.
- Do NOT drink untreated wild water if any purification method is available.
- Do NOT ignore dark urine — it signals urgent need for fluid replacement.
