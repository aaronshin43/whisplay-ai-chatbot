Category: Spinal Injury

STEPS:
1. Call 911 immediately.
2. Tell the person not to move — keep them completely still.
3. Kneel at the person's head and place both hands gently on either side of the head to stabilize it in the position found.
4. Do not attempt to straighten the neck or spine — hold it in the position you found it.
5. Keep applying manual stabilization until emergency services arrive and take over.
6. If the person must be moved due to immediate danger, use the log-roll technique with at least 3 people to maintain spinal alignment.
7. If unconscious and not breathing, airway management takes priority — perform jaw thrust without tilting the head.
8. Cover with a blanket to prevent hypothermia.

NEVER DO:
- Do NOT move the person unless there is immediate danger to their life.
- Do NOT tilt the head or attempt to correct deformity.
- Do NOT remove a helmet if the person is breathing — leave it on.
- Do NOT allow the person to walk or sit up.
