Category: Poisonous Plant Exposure

STEPS:
1. For skin contact with poison ivy, oak, or sumac: wash the area with soap and water immediately — acting within 10 minutes greatly reduces the reaction.
2. Remove contaminated clothing carefully without touching the outer surface.
3. Apply cool compresses to reduce itching; calamine lotion or hydrocortisone cream helps.
4. Do NOT scratch — scratching spreads the irritant and introduces infection.
5. For plant or berry ingestion: call 911 immediately and identify the plant if possible.
6. Do NOT induce vomiting unless emergency services specifically instruct you to.
7. For eye exposure: flush with clean water for 15-20 minutes without rubbing.
8. Monitor for throat swelling or breathing difficulty — these require emergency care.

NEVER DO:
- Do NOT rub eyes after touching any plant — resin transfers easily from hands to eyes.
- Do NOT induce vomiting after plant ingestion without professional guidance.
- Do NOT burn poison ivy — inhaling the smoke causes severe airway injury.
- Do NOT scratch blisters — broken skin leads to infection.
