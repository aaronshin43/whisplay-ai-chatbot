Category: Pediatric Emergency

STEPS:
1. Call 911 immediately.
2. Infant CPR (under 1 year): 2 fingers on center of chest, compress 4 cm deep at 100-120 per minute.
3. Infant rescue breaths: tilt head only slightly, cover both mouth and nose, give small gentle puffs.
4. Give 30 compressions then 2 breaths — same ratio as adult CPR.
5. Child CPR (1 year to puberty): one or two hands on chest, compress one-third depth.
6. Infant choking: 5 back blows between shoulder blades, then 5 chest thrusts — not abdominal thrusts.
7. Febrile seizure: do not restrain, protect from injury, place on side, call if seizure exceeds 5 minutes.
8. Stay calm — a calm caregiver reduces the child's distress.

NEVER DO:
- Do NOT perform abdominal thrusts on an infant under 1 year.
- Do NOT tilt an infant's head too far back — overextension blocks the airway.
- Do NOT give aspirin to a child — risk of Reye syndrome.
- Do NOT leave a seizing child alone.
