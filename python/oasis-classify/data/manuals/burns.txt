Category: Burns

STEPS:
1. Remove the person from the heat source or stop the burning immediately.
2. Cool the burn with cool (not cold or icy) running water for at least 20 minutes.
3. Remove jewelry and clothing near the burned area before swelling — do not remove clothing stuck to the burn.
4. Cover the cooled burn with a clean non-fluffy material (cling film, clean plastic bag, or clean cloth).
5. Call emergency services for burns larger than the person's palm, deep burns, or burns on face, hands, feet, or genitals.
6. Keep the person warm — prolonged cooling can cause hypothermia.
7. Give sips of water if the person is conscious and burns are significant.
8. For chemical burns, flush continuously with water and call 911.

NEVER DO:
- Do NOT apply ice, butter, toothpaste, or oils — they trap heat and increase infection risk.
- Do NOT pop burn blisters — they protect the wound.
- Do NOT use fluffy cotton wool — it sticks to the wound.
- Do NOT remove clothing stuck to burned skin.
