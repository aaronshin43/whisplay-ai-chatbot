Category: CPR / Cardiac Arrest

STEPS:
1. Check for safety, tap the person's shoulder and shout to confirm unresponsiveness.
2. Call 911 immediately or have someone else call.
3. Tilt the head back, lift the chin, check for breathing for no more than 10 seconds.
4. If not breathing normally, place the heel of one hand on center of chest, interlock fingers.
5. Push down hard and fast — compress at least 5 cm deep at 100-120 compressions per minute.
6. After 30 compressions, give 2 rescue breaths: tilt head, pinch nose, seal mouth, breathe until chest rises.
7. Continue 30 compressions then 2 breaths without stopping until AED arrives or services take over.
8. If an AED is available, turn it on and follow all voice prompts immediately.

NEVER DO:
- Do NOT stop compressions repeatedly to check for a pulse.
- Do NOT tilt the head if spinal injury is suspected — use jaw thrust instead.
- Do NOT compress too slowly or shallowly — depth and rate are critical for blood flow.
