Category: Acute Stress Reaction

STEPS:
1. Ensure the person is physically safe and removed from any ongoing danger before addressing psychological symptoms.
2. Introduce yourself calmly. Use their name if known. Speak slowly and use simple, short sentences.
3. Do not force them to talk about or relive what happened. Simply be present.
4. Offer basic comfort: warmth, water, a blanket. Physical comfort anchors awareness.
5. Use gentle grounding if they seem dissociated or confused: ask them to describe what they can see or feel around them right now.
6. Monitor for signs of dissociation — blank staring, unresponsiveness, repetitive movements — and stay close.
7. Help connect them with emergency services, a mental health professional, or a trusted person as soon as possible. Call 911 if they are unable to care for themselves.

NEVER DO:
- Do NOT pressure them to describe or process the traumatic event.
- Do NOT leave someone who is severely dissociated or unresponsive alone.
- Do NOT tell them how they should be feeling or that they will be fine.
- Do NOT minimize what they witnessed or experienced.
