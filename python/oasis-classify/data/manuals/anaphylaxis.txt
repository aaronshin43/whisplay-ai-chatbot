Category: Anaphylaxis / Severe Allergic Reaction

STEPS:
1. Call 911 immediately — anaphylaxis is life-threatening.
2. If an epinephrine auto-injector (EpiPen) is available, administer it into the outer thigh without delay.
3. Lay the person flat and elevate their legs unless they have difficulty breathing.
4. If breathing is difficult, allow them to sit upright in a position of comfort.
5. A second dose of epinephrine can be given 5-15 minutes after the first if symptoms persist.
6. Do not let the person stand or walk — keep them still.
7. If the person becomes unresponsive and stops breathing, begin CPR.
8. Even if symptoms improve, transport to hospital — biphasic reactions can occur hours later.

NEVER DO:
- Do NOT delay epinephrine waiting to see if symptoms improve — use it immediately.
- Do NOT give antihistamines as a substitute for epinephrine — they do not treat anaphylaxis.
- Do NOT leave the person alone.
- Do NOT allow them to stand if dizzy or blood pressure is low.
