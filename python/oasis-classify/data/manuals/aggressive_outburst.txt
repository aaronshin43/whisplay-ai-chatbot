Category: Aggressive Outburst

STEPS:
1. Prioritize your own safety first. If you feel physically threatened at any moment, leave the area and call 911.
2. Remain calm. Keep your voice low, slow, and even. Do not raise your voice or match their energy.
3. Give the person physical space — do not stand close, block an exit, or crowd them. Position yourself near a door.
4. Avoid direct prolonged eye contact, which can be perceived as a challenge or threat.
5. Use short, non-confrontational sentences: I can see you are very upset. I want to help.
6. Do not argue, issue ultimatums, or try to physically restrain them unless someone is in immediate danger.
7. If aggression escalates to physical violence or you cannot safely de-escalate, call 911 immediately and move others away from the area.

NEVER DO:
- Do NOT corner the person or block their exit.
- Do NOT shout, argue, or challenge their statements.
- Do NOT attempt physical restraint unless trained to do so and there is immediate danger.
- Do NOT leave vulnerable bystanders exposed to harm.
