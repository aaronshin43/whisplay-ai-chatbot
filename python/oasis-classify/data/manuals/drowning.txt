Category: Drowning / Near-Drowning

STEPS:
1. Ensure your own safety before entering water — throw a rope, lifebuoy, or extend a branch from shore.
2. Call 911 immediately.
3. Once the person is on shore, check for responsiveness.
4. If not breathing, begin rescue breathing immediately — give 5 initial rescue breaths.
5. Start CPR: 30 compressions, 2 rescue breaths. Continue until emergency services arrive.
6. If the person is breathing, place in the recovery position on their side to allow water to drain.
7. Keep the person warm — drowning victims lose heat rapidly.
8. All drowning victims must be evaluated at hospital even if they appear to recover fully.

NEVER DO:
- Do NOT attempt to drain water from the lungs before starting CPR — begin breathing support immediately.
- Do NOT assume the person is safe because they are breathing — secondary drowning can occur hours later.
- Do NOT leave the person alone after rescue.
- Do NOT compress the abdomen to expel water.
