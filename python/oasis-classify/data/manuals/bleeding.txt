Category: Severe Bleeding / Hemorrhage Control

STEPS:
1. Call 911 immediately.
2. Apply DIRECT PRESSURE to the wound using any clean cloth or your bare hands.
3. Press hard and do not release — maintain continuous pressure for at least 10 minutes.
4. If blood soaks through, add more material on top without removing the first layer.
5. If bleeding is from a limb and direct pressure fails, apply a tourniquet 5-7 cm above the wound.
6. Tighten until bleeding stops completely and note the exact time applied.
7. Lay the person flat and elevate the limb above heart level if no spinal injury is suspected.
8. Monitor for shock: pale cold clammy skin, rapid weak pulse, confusion.

NEVER DO:
- Do NOT remove an embedded object from the wound — press around it instead.
- Do NOT apply a tourniquet over a joint.
- Do NOT remove the first cloth layer to inspect — it disrupts clotting.
- Do NOT use a belt as a tourniquet — too wide to tighten effectively.
