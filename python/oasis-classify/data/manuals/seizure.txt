Category: Seizure / Epilepsy

STEPS:
1. Call emergency services (911) if the seizure lasts more than 5 minutes, the person does not regain consciousness, or it is their first known seizure.
2. Protect the person from injury: clear hard objects away and place something soft under their head.
3. Time the seizure from the beginning.
4. Gently turn the person onto their side (recovery position) to keep the airway clear.
5. Stay with the person and keep bystanders at a safe distance.
6. After the seizure, speak calmly and reassure them — they may be confused for several minutes.
7. Check for breathing and pulse after the seizure stops.
8. If not breathing after the seizure, begin CPR.

NEVER DO:
- Do NOT hold the person down or restrain them during a seizure.
- Do NOT put anything in their mouth — they cannot swallow their tongue.
- Do NOT give food or drink until the person is fully alert.
- Do NOT leave the person alone until they are fully conscious and oriented.
