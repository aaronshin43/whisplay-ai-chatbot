Category: Head Injury / Concussion

STEPS:
1. Call emergency services (911) if the person lost consciousness, is confused, has unequal pupils, is vomiting repeatedly, or has a visible skull deformity.
2. Keep the person still and calm — minimize movement of the head and neck.
3. Control any scalp bleeding with gentle direct pressure using a clean cloth.
4. Do not apply firm pressure if you suspect a skull fracture — use very light pressure only.
5. Monitor level of consciousness: speak to them and note their alertness and responses.
6. If conscious, ask for their name, date, and location to assess orientation.
7. If the person loses consciousness but is breathing, place in the recovery position.
8. If not breathing, begin CPR immediately.

NEVER DO:
- Do NOT leave the person alone for at least a few hours after a head injury.
- Do NOT give the person alcohol, sedatives, or pain medication that can mask symptoms.
- Do NOT remove a helmet at the scene unless needed for CPR.
- Do NOT assume all is well because the person feels fine — symptoms can appear hours later.
