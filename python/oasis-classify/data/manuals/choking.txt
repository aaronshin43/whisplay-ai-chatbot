Category: Choking / Airway Obstruction

STEPS:
1. Ask "Are you choking?" — if they cannot speak, cough, or breathe, act immediately.
2. Call 911 or have someone call while you act.
3. Lean them forward and give 5 firm back blows between the shoulder blades with the heel of your hand.
4. If back blows fail, give 5 abdominal thrusts: stand behind them, fist just above navel, thrust inward and upward.
5. Alternate 5 back blows with 5 abdominal thrusts until object is expelled or person loses consciousness.
6. If the person becomes unconscious, lower them to the floor and begin CPR.
7. Each time you open the airway for rescue breaths, look for the object and remove only if clearly visible.
8. For pregnant or obese adults, use chest thrusts instead of abdominal thrusts.

NEVER DO:
- Do NOT perform a blind finger sweep — only remove an object if you can clearly see it.
- Do NOT slap the back while upright — lean them forward first.
- Do NOT give abdominal thrusts to infants — use back blows and chest thrusts only.
