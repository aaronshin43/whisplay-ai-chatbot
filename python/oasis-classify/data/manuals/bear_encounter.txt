Category: Bear Encounter / Bear Attack

STEPS:
1. Do NOT run — running triggers a bear's chase instinct.
2. Identify the bear: grizzly bears have a shoulder hump; black bears are smaller.
3. Grizzly attack: fall face-down, cover the back of your neck with clasped hands, spread your legs wide. Play dead until the bear leaves.
4. Stay completely still and silent — grizzly attacks are defensive and stop when the bear feels safe.
5. Black bear attack: do NOT play dead. Fight back hard — strike the nose and eyes.
6. Bear has not charged: back away slowly, speak in a calm low voice, avoid eye contact.
7. Use bear spray at 8-10 meters as the bear charges — aim for the face.
8. After the attack: treat wounds and call 911 when safe.

NEVER DO:
- Do NOT run from any bear — you cannot outrun one.
- Do NOT play dead with a black bear — fight back instead.
- Do NOT climb a tree near a black bear — they climb well.
- Do NOT approach a bear cub — the mother is always nearby.
