Category: Chest Wound / Pneumothorax

STEPS:
1. Call emergency services (911) immediately.
2. If the wound is sucking (gurgling or hissing with breathing), seal it immediately.
3. Apply a chest seal or improvise with plastic wrap: tape three sides on exhale to allow air out but not in.
4. Help the person into the position that makes breathing easiest — usually sitting or semi-reclined.
5. Monitor breathing closely — watch for worsening difficulty or increased distress.
6. If using a three-sided seal and the person worsens, briefly lift the unsealed side to release trapped air.
7. If unresponsive, lay down and begin CPR if not breathing.
8. Keep the person calm and still to minimize oxygen demand.

NEVER DO:
- Do NOT leave a sucking chest wound open and unsealed.
- Do NOT apply a fully airtight seal without a valve — trapped air can cause tension pneumothorax.
- Do NOT remove impaled objects from the chest — stabilize them in place.
- Do NOT lay the person flat if conscious and breathing — sitting upright is safer.
