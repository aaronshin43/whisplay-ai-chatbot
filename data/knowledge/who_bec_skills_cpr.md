# WHO BEC and Red Cross CPR Protocol for Adult and Child

## Initiation Criteria for Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation (CPR)
- Patient is unconscious, not breathing (or only gasping), and lacks a clear pulse: Start CPR immediately without delay to prevent a 7 to 10% survival reduction per minute.

## Adult CPR Execution Protocol
- Unsafe scene condition: Verify scene safety before approaching the patient.
- Patient responsiveness check requirement: Tap the patient's shoulders firmly and shout to assess responsiveness.
- Unresponsive patient condition: Call emergency services (911/999/112) immediately and explicitly delegate a bystander to retrieve an AED.
- Standard airway opening requirement: Execute the head-tilt chin-lift maneuver by placing one hand on the forehead, tilting the head back, and lifting the chin up.
- Suspected cervical trauma condition: Execute the jaw thrust maneuver only and strictly avoid tilting the head.
- Breathing assessment requirement: Look, listen, and feel for normal breathing for a maximum of 10 seconds.
- Occasional gasping observation: Classify gasping as abnormal breathing and begin CPR immediately.
- Chest compression hand placement: Kneel beside the patient, place the heel of one hand on the centre of the lower half of the sternum, place the second hand on top, and interlock fingers while keeping them off the ribs.
- Chest compression posture requirement: Keep arms perfectly straight and position your shoulders directly over your hands.
- Chest compression execution: Push down firmly 5 to 6 cm (2 to 2.5 inches) at a strict rate of 100 to 120 compressions per minute.
- Post-compression recoil requirement: Allow the chest to fully recoil between every compression and do not lean on the chest.
- Rescue breath preparation: Maintain the airway opening maneuver, pinch the nose closed, and create an airtight seal over the patient's mouth.
- Rescue breath execution: Blow steadily for exactly 1 second, watch for visible chest rise, remove your mouth to allow the chest to fall, and immediately deliver a second breath.
- Unsuccessful chest rise condition: Reposition the patient's head and retry the rescue breath strictly one time.
- Standard CPR cycle maintenance: Alternate 30 chest compressions followed by 2 rescue breaths continuously without stopping.
- Multiple rescuer condition: Switch the active compressor every 2 minutes to prevent fatigue.
- Untrained or unwilling rescuer condition: Execute compression-only CPR continuously without delivering rescue breaths.
- AED availability condition: Turn on the AED immediately, follow audio instructions, attach pads to the bare chest (one below the right collarbone, one under the left armpit), and ensure no physical contact with the patient during analysis or shock delivery.
- Post-shock AED condition: Resume chest compressions immediately after each delivered shock.
- CPR termination condition: Continue all CPR activities until emergency services take over, the patient shows clear signs of life (normal breathing or movement), or you become physically too exhausted to safely continue.

## Child CPR Execution Protocol (1 Year to Puberty)
- Standard child CPR cycle: Alternate 30 chest compressions followed by 2 rescue breaths continuously.
- Child chest compression execution: Push down approximately 5 cm (about one-third of the total chest depth) at a strict rate of 100 to 120 compressions per minute.
- Child chest compression hand placement: Use only one hand (or two fingers for an infant) to deliver compressions.
- Known drowning or respiratory arrest condition: Deliver 2 rescue breaths immediately before starting any chest compressions.

## Prohibited Actions During CPR Execution
- Pulse check restriction: Do not stop chest compressions to check for a pulse except during the designated 2-minute rescuer swap intervals.
- Compression rate restriction: Do not compress slower than 100 compressions per minute or faster than 120 compressions per minute.
- Compression depth restriction: Do not compress shallower than 5 cm to avoid ineffective blood circulation.
- Chest recoil restriction: Do not lean on the chest between compressions as it prevents necessary cardiac refilling.
- Preparation delay restriction: Do not delay the initiation of chest compressions to search for protective equipment like masks or gloves.---
