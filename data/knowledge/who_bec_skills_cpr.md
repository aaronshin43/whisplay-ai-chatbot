# WHO BEC / Red Cross — CPR Protocol (Adult & Child)

**Source:** WHO Basic Emergency Care (BEC) 2018 + American Red Cross First Aid Guidelines

## When to Start CPR

Start CPR immediately if the person is:
- **Unconscious** (unresponsive to tapping and shouting)
- **Not breathing** OR only gasping
- **No pulse** (or you are unsure)

**Do not delay.** Every minute without CPR reduces survival by 7–10%.

## Adult CPR — Step-by-Step

### Step 1 — Ensure Scene Safety
Check that the scene is safe before approaching.

### Step 2 — Check Responsiveness
Tap shoulders firmly and shout: **"Are you okay? Can you hear me?"**

### Step 3 — Call Emergency Services
Call 911 / 999 / 112 immediately. If others are present, delegate: "You — call 911 now."
If an AED is available nearby, send someone to retrieve it.

### Step 4 — Open the Airway
Use **head-tilt chin-lift**: place one hand on forehead, tilt head back, lift chin up.
(If trauma suspected, use jaw thrust only — do NOT tilt head.)

### Step 5 — Check Breathing
Look, listen, and feel for no more than **10 seconds**.
Occasional gasps are NOT normal breathing — begin CPR.

### Step 6 — Begin Chest Compressions (30 compressions)
1. Kneel beside the person.
2. Place the **heel of one hand on the centre of the chest** (lower half of the sternum/breastbone).
3. Place your other hand on top and interlock fingers. Keep fingers off the ribs.
4. Keep arms **straight**; position shoulders directly over hands.
5. Push down **5–6 cm** (2–2.5 inches) — firm, hard compressions.
6. **Rate: 100–120 compressions per minute** (roughly 2 per second).
7. Allow the chest to **fully recoil** between compressions — do not lean on the chest.

### Step 7 — Give 2 Rescue Breaths
1. Maintain head-tilt chin-lift.
2. **Pinch the nose** closed with your fingers.
3. Create an airtight seal over the mouth.
4. Blow steadily for **1 second** — watch for chest rise.
5. Remove your mouth; allow chest to fall.
6. Give a **second breath**.
7. If chest does not rise: reposition head and retry once.

### Step 8 — Continue 30:2 Cycle
- Alternate **30 compressions → 2 breaths** without stopping.
- If two rescuers: switch compressor every **2 minutes** to avoid fatigue.
- **Compression-only CPR** (no breaths) is acceptable if you are untrained or unwilling to give breaths.

### Step 9 — Use AED as Soon as Available
- Turn on the AED and follow its voice instructions.
- Attach pads to bare chest: one below right collarbone, one under left armpit.
- Ensure no one touches the person when AED analyzes or shocks.
- Resume CPR immediately after each shock.

### Step 10 — Continue Until
- Emergency services arrive and take over.
- The person shows clear signs of life (normal breathing, movement).
- You are too exhausted to continue safely.

## Child CPR (1 year to puberty)

Same 30:2 ratio. Key differences:
- **Depth: 5 cm** (about one-third of chest depth)
- Use **one hand** (or two fingers for infant) for compressions
- Give **2 rescue breaths before starting** if drowning or respiratory cause is known
- Rate: **100–120 per minute** (same)

---

## Key Numbers — Quick Reference

| Parameter | Adult | Child |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Compression depth | 5–6 cm | ~5 cm |
| Compression rate | 100–120 / min | 100–120 / min |
| Ratio (compressions:breaths) | 30:2 | 30:2 |
| Breaths duration | 1 second each | 1 second each |

---

## What NOT To Do

- Do NOT stop compressions to check for pulse (except every 2 min)
- Do NOT compress too slowly (< 100/min) or too fast (> 120/min)
- Do NOT compress too shallow (< 5 cm) — ineffective
- Do NOT lean on chest between compressions — prevents recoil
- Do NOT delay starting compressions to find a mask or gloves
