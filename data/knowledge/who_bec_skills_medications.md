## Medication Administration Skill Discussion

The medications below represent a basic set for emergency conditions. Always check locally available drug concentrations. Most conditions will require additional treatments beyond these.

---

## Adrenaline (Epinephrine)
**Indication:** Anaphylaxis / severe allergic reaction; severe wheezing
**Form:** Solution 1 mg in 1 ml ampoule (1:1000)
**Route:** Intramuscular (IM) — preferred site: outer mid-thigh
**Dose:**
- Adults ≥ 50 kg: 0.5 mg IM (0.5 ml of 1:1000). May repeat at 5-minute intervals.
- Adults 40 kg: 0.4 mg IM; 30 kg: 0.3 mg IM.
- Paediatrics anaphylaxis: 0.15 mg IM (0.15 ml), repeat every 5–15 minutes as needed.
- Paediatrics severe asthma: 0.01 mg/kg IM up to 0.3 mg, repeat every 15 minutes.
**Adverse effects:** anxiety, palpitations, tachycardia, dizziness, sweating, nausea, hyperglycaemia, chest pain, hypertension, tissue necrosis at injection site.

---

## Antibiotics
**Indication:** Life-threatening infections (empirical use before source identified)
**Dose:** Determined by local treatment protocols. Broad-spectrum regimen appropriate for very ill patients.
**Adverse effects:** allergic reactions, gastrointestinal upset; specific effects vary by antibiotic.

---

## Acetylsalicylic Acid (Aspirin)
**Indication:** Suspected heart attack
**Form:** Tablet 100 mg, 300 mg
**Dose:** 300 mg oral (chewed or dispersed in water) — single immediate dose
**Do NOT give aspirin if:**
- Any active bleeding
- Chest pain that is sudden, maximum at onset, sharp and tearing, radiating to the back (possible aortic tear)
**Adverse effects:** gastrointestinal irritation with blood loss, tinnitus, anaphylaxis.

---

## Benzodiazepines — Diazepam
**Indication:** Seizures / convulsions
**Form:** Tablet 2 mg, 5 mg; Solution 5 mg/1 ml ampoule
**Dose — Adults:**
- First dose: 10 mg slow IV push OR 20 mg rectally.
- Second dose (after 10 minutes): 5 mg slow IV push or 10 mg rectally.
- Maximum IV dose: 30 mg.
**Dose — Children:**
- First dose: 0.2 mg/kg slow IV push or 0.5 mg/kg rectally.
- Can repeat half of first dose after 10 minutes if seizures continue.
- Maximum IV dose: 20 mg.
- Do NOT give second dose if respiratory rate < 10 breaths/min.
- Do NOT give diazepam intramuscularly (unpredictable absorption).
**Adverse effects:** sedation, respiratory depression, hypotension, bradycardia, nausea, vomiting.

---

## Glucose (Dextrose)
**Indication:** Hypoglycaemia (low blood sugar)
**Form:** D50 (50%), D25 (25%), D10 (10%) solutions
**Dose — Adults and children > 40 kg:** 25–50 ml IV D50, or 125–250 ml IV D10.
**Dose — Children ≤ 40 kg:** 5 ml/kg IV D10 (preferred).
**If no IV access:** 2–5 ml of 50% dextrose in buccal space, or 1 level teaspoon of sugar moistened with water every 10–20 minutes in buccal space.
**CAUTION:** NEVER give dextrose intramuscularly (serious tissue damage). D10 preferred in children < 40 kg.
**Adverse effects:** hyperglycaemia, dizziness, skin necrosis if injected outside the vein.

---

## Magnesium Sulphate
**Indication:** Eclampsia or pregnant woman with seizure/convulsion
**Dose — Loading (IV + IM):**
- 4 g IV (dilute to 20% solution; give 20 ml slowly over 20 minutes) PLUS
- 10 g IM (5 g with 1 ml of 2% lidocaine in each upper outer buttock quadrant).
**If unable to give IV:** 10 g IM only (5 g in each buttock).
**If seizures recur:** after 15 minutes, give 2 g (10 ml of 20%) IV over 20 minutes.
**Maintenance:** 5 g of 50% solution IM with 1 ml of 2% lidocaine every 4 hours in alternate buttocks.
**STOP if:** respiratory rate < 16/min; loss of knee jerk reflex; urine output < 100 ml/4 hours.
**Adverse effects:** hypotension, respiratory depression, drowsiness, confusion, muscle weakness, nausea, flushed skin.

---

## Naloxone
**Indication:** Opioid overdose
**Form:** 400 mcg/ml in 1 ml ampoule
**Dose:** IV: 100 mcg single dose; IM: 400 mcg single dose. May repeat every 5 minutes as needed.
**Note:** Naloxone effects last only 1–3 hours. Many opioids are longer-acting — may need continuous infusion at 0.4 mg/hour. Any patient treated with naloxone must be monitored closely.
**Adverse effects:** hypertension, cardiac arrhythmias, hyperventilation, difficulty breathing, agitation.

---

## Oxytocin
**Indication:** Treatment of postpartum haemorrhage
**Form:** 10 IU in 1 ml ampoule
**Initial dose:** 10 IU IM AND start IV fluids with 20 IU/L at 60 drops/minute.
**Once placenta delivered:** continue IV fluids with 20 IU/L at 30 drops/minute if still bleeding.
**If placenta requires manual removal or uterus does not contract:** repeat 10 IU IM; continue IV fluids 20 IU/L at 20 drops/minute for 1 hour after bleeding stops.
**Maximum:** 3 L of IV fluids containing oxytocin.
**Adverse effects:** nausea/vomiting, headache, rash, anaphylaxis, uterine spasm.

---

## Paracetamol (Acetaminophen)
**Indication:** Mild to moderate pain, fever, headache
**Form:** Tablet 250 mg, 500 mg; Rectal suppositories 250 mg, 500 mg
**Dose — Adults:** 500 mg–1 g every 6 hours; max 4 g/day (max 2 g/day with liver impairment).
**Dose — Children:** 10–15 mg/kg up to six times a day.
**Adverse effects:** rash, liver damage following overdoses.

---

## Salbutamol (Albuterol)
**Indication:** Severe wheezing
**Form:** Inhaler 100 mcg/puff with spacer, or nebulizer
**Dose — Adults (inhaler + spacer):** 2 puffs every 2 minutes until improved (after priming with 5 puffs).
**Dose — Children < 5 years:** 6 puffs total via spacer; children > 5 years: 12 puffs total. Repeat regularly until improved. In severe cases, can give 6 or 12 puffs several times in an hour.
**Nebulizer:** Adult 5 mg in 5 ml saline; Child 2.5 mg in 3 ml saline.
**Adverse effects:** palpitations, fine tremor, headache, tachycardia.

---

## Tetanus Vaccination
**Indication:** All injured children whose vaccinations are not up to date; all adults not vaccinated in past 5 years; if immunization status unknown.
**Form:** Solution 5 units per 0.5 ml
**Dose:** 0.5 ml IM
**Adverse effects:** pain at injection site, allergic reaction, fever, nausea.
