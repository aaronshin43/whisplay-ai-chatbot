# WHO BEC Skills Emergency Medications Reference

## Adrenaline (Epinephrine) Administration Protocol
- Indication for use: Administer for anaphylaxis, severe allergic reactions, or severe wheezing.
- Medication formulation: Use a solution of 1 mg in a 1 ml ampoule (1:1000 concentration).
- Administration route: Administer intramuscularly (IM), preferring the outer mid-thigh as the injection site.
- Adult patient weighing 50 kg or more: Administer 0.5 mg IM (0.5 ml of 1:1000) and repeat at 5-minute intervals if necessary.
- Adult patient weighing 40 kg: Administer 0.4 mg IM.
- Adult patient weighing 30 kg: Administer 0.3 mg IM.
- Paediatric anaphylaxis condition: Administer 0.15 mg IM (0.15 ml) and repeat every 5 to 15 minutes as needed.
- Paediatric severe asthma condition: Administer 0.01 mg/kg IM up to a maximum dose of 0.3 mg and repeat every 15 minutes.
- Adverse effects monitoring: Monitor for anxiety, palpitations, tachycardia, dizziness, sweating, nausea, hyperglycaemia, chest pain, hypertension, and tissue necrosis at the injection site.

## Antibiotics Administration Protocol
- Indication for use: Administer empirical broad-spectrum antibiotics for life-threatening infections before the source is identified.
- Dosing requirement: Determine the exact dose and regimen strictly based on local treatment protocols appropriate for very ill patients.
- Adverse effects monitoring: Monitor for allergic reactions, gastrointestinal upset, and specific effects that vary by the chosen antibiotic.

## Acetylsalicylic Acid (Aspirin) Administration Protocol
- Indication for use: Administer for suspected heart attack.
- Medication formulation: Use 100 mg or 300 mg tablets.
- Standard adult dosing: Administer a single immediate dose of 300 mg orally (chewed or dispersed in water).
- Active bleeding condition: Do strictly NOT give aspirin.
- Sudden, sharp, tearing chest pain radiating to the back (possible aortic tear) condition: Do strictly NOT give aspirin.
- Adverse effects monitoring: Monitor for gastrointestinal irritation with blood loss, tinnitus, and anaphylaxis.

## Benzodiazepines (Diazepam) Administration Protocol
- Indication for use: Administer for seizures or convulsions.
- Medication formulation: Use 2 mg or 5 mg tablets, or a 5 mg/1 ml ampoule solution.
- Adult first dose administration: Administer 10 mg via slow IV push OR 20 mg rectally.
- Adult second dose administration: Administer 5 mg via slow IV push OR 10 mg rectally if seizures continue after 10 minutes.
- Adult maximum IV dose limit: Do not exceed 30 mg total IV dose.
- Paediatric first dose administration: Administer 0.2 mg/kg via slow IV push OR 0.5 mg/kg rectally.
- Paediatric second dose administration: Repeat half of the first dose if seizures continue after 10 minutes.
- Paediatric maximum IV dose limit: Do not exceed 20 mg total IV dose.
- Respiratory rate under 10 breaths per minute condition: Do strictly NOT administer a second dose.
- Intramuscular administration restriction: Do strictly NOT give diazepam intramuscularly due to unpredictable absorption rates.
- Adverse effects monitoring: Monitor for sedation, respiratory depression, hypotension, bradycardia, nausea, and vomiting.

## Glucose (Dextrose) Administration Protocol
- Indication for use: Administer for hypoglycaemia (low blood sugar).
- Medication formulation: Use D50 (50%), D25 (25%), or D10 (10%) solutions.
- Adult or child over 40 kg dosing: Administer 25 to 50 ml IV D50, OR 125 to 250 ml IV D10.
- Child 40 kg or under dosing: Administer 5 ml/kg IV D10 (preferred concentration for this weight class).
- No IV access available condition: Administer 2 to 5 ml of 50% dextrose in the buccal space, or place 1 level teaspoon of sugar moistened with water in the buccal space every 10 to 20 minutes.
- Intramuscular administration restriction: NEVER administer dextrose intramuscularly to prevent serious tissue damage.
- Adverse effects monitoring: Monitor for hyperglycaemia, dizziness, and skin necrosis if injected outside the vein.

## Magnesium Sulphate Administration Protocol
- Indication for use: Administer for eclampsia or a pregnant woman presenting with a seizure/convulsion.
- Loading dose administration (IV and IM combined): Administer 4 g IV (diluted to a 20% solution and given slowly as 20 ml over 20 minutes) PLUS 10 g IM (given as 5 g mixed with 1 ml of 2% lidocaine in each upper outer buttock quadrant).
- Unable to establish IV access condition: Administer 10 g IM only (5 g in each buttock).
- Seizure recurrence after 15 minutes condition: Administer an additional 2 g (10 ml of 20% solution) IV slowly over 20 minutes.
- Maintenance dose administration: Administer 5 g of 50% solution IM mixed with 1 ml of 2% lidocaine every 4 hours in alternate buttocks.
- Administration stop condition: Stop administration immediately if the respiratory rate falls below 16 breaths per minute, the knee jerk reflex is lost, or urine output falls below 100 ml per 4 hours.
- Adverse effects monitoring: Monitor for hypotension, respiratory depression, drowsiness, confusion, muscle weakness, nausea, and flushed skin.

## Naloxone Administration Protocol
- Indication for use: Administer for opioid overdose.
- Medication formulation: Use a 400 mcg/ml solution in a 1 ml ampoule.
- Standard dosing: Administer a single dose of 100 mcg IV OR 400 mcg IM, and repeat every 5 minutes as needed.
- Long-acting opioid overdose consideration: Initiate a continuous infusion at 0.4 mg/hour and monitor closely, as the effects of naloxone only last 1 to 3 hours.
- Adverse effects monitoring: Monitor for hypertension, cardiac arrhythmias, hyperventilation, difficulty breathing, and agitation.

## Oxytocin Administration Protocol
- Indication for use: Administer for the treatment of postpartum haemorrhage.
- Medication formulation: Use 10 IU in a 1 ml ampoule.
- Initial dose administration: Administer 10 IU IM AND start IV fluids containing 20 IU per litre running at 60 drops per minute.
- Persistent bleeding after placenta delivery condition: Continue IV fluids containing 20 IU per litre at a reduced rate of 30 drops per minute.
- Placenta requires manual removal or uterus does not contract condition: Repeat 10 IU IM dose AND continue IV fluids containing 20 IU per litre at 20 drops per minute for exactly 1 hour after bleeding stops.
- Maximum dose limit: Do not exceed a maximum of 3 litres of IV fluids containing oxytocin.
- Adverse effects monitoring: Monitor for nausea, vomiting, headache, rash, anaphylaxis, and uterine spasm.

## Paracetamol (Acetaminophen) Administration Protocol
- Indication for use: Administer for mild to moderate pain, fever, or headache.
- Medication formulation: Use 250 mg or 500 mg tablets, or 250 mg or 500 mg rectal suppositories.
- Adult standard dosing: Administer 500 mg to 1 g every 6 hours, up to a maximum of 4 g per day.
- Adult liver impairment condition: Restrict the maximum dose to 2 g per day.
- Paediatric standard dosing: Administer 10 to 15 mg/kg up to six times a day.
- Adverse effects monitoring: Monitor for rash and liver damage following potential overdoses.

## Salbutamol (Albuterol) Administration Protocol
- Indication for use: Administer for severe wheezing.
- Medication formulation: Use an inhaler (100 mcg/puff) with a spacer, or a nebulizer.
- Adult inhaler dosing: Prime the inhaler with 5 puffs, then administer 2 puffs via spacer every 2 minutes until symptoms improve.
- Child under 5 years inhaler dosing: Administer 6 puffs total via spacer and repeat regularly until improved.
- Child over 5 years inhaler dosing: Administer 12 puffs total via spacer and repeat regularly until improved.
- Severe paediatric wheezing condition: Administer 6 or 12 puffs several times within a single hour.
- Adult nebulizer dosing: Administer 5 mg mixed in 5 ml saline.
- Paediatric nebulizer dosing: Administer 2.5 mg mixed in 3 ml saline.
- Adverse effects monitoring: Monitor for palpitations, fine tremor, headache, and tachycardia.

## Tetanus Vaccination Administration Protocol
- Indication for use: Administer to any injured child with outdated vaccinations, any adult not vaccinated in the past 5 years, or any patient with unknown immunization status.
- Medication formulation: Use a solution of 5 units per 0.5 ml.
- Standard dosing: Administer 0.5 ml IM.
- Adverse effects monitoring: Monitor for pain at the injection site, allergic reaction, fever, and nausea.
