# Wounds, Bleeding, and Related Emergencies

## Bleeding Control
**Types:** Arterial (spurting, life-threatening), Venous (smooth flow), Internal (bruising). 
**Management Steps:**
1. **Direct Pressure:** Apply firm pressure directly to the source using gauze or a clean pad (2-3 fingers). If blood-soaked, add another pad on top; do not remove the original.
2. **Deep Wound Packing:** If simple pressure fails on a deep/gaping wound, pack the wound completely with clean material/gauze, then apply pressure over it. *Do not leave packing >24 hours.*
3. **Tourniquet Application:** Use ONLY if other measures fail and bleeding is life-threatening. Apply on extremities only, above the bleeding site (prefer upper arm/leg), using a wide band. Tighten until bleeding stops. **Record exact application time.** *Never loosen a tourniquet in the pre-hospital setting.*

## Wound Cleaning & Dressing
Clean wounds only after bleeding is controlled.
1. Wash hands and apply gloves. Wash the skin around the wound.
2. Remove large debris with tweezers.
3. **Irrigation:** Flush the wound vigorously with at least 1 Liter of clean/disinfected water under pressure (e.g., using a syringe or punctured bottle cap). *Do not use hydrogen peroxide, alcohol, or iodine.*
4. **Closure:** If a laceration gapes > ½ inch, is exceptionally dirty, or is an animal bite, *leave it open* and pack with gauze to allow drainage. For clean, small cuts, use wound closure tape strips. Apply wound gel and a sterile dressing.
5. Check perfusion (capillary refill, distal pulses, sensation) before and after dressing.

## Managing Specific Injuries
- **Impaled Objects:** Do NOT remove unless it directly prevents CPR. Immobilize the object with bulky dressings.
- **Friction Blisters:** If opening is necessary, sterilize a needle, puncture wide, and *leave the blister roof intact*. Create a friction-limiting "donut" dressing.
- **Nosebleeds:** Sit leaning forward, pinch the soft fleshy part of the nose continuously for 10 minutes. If bleeding persists, pack with decongestant-soaked gauze. *If blood runs down the throat, evacuate rapidly.*
- **Knocked-Out Tooth:** Handle by the crown only. Rinse (do not scrub). Try to press back into the socket. If unsuccessful, store in milk, 0.9% saline, or the person's mouth.

## Wound Infection
- **Mild:** Pain, redness, light pus. Re-clean, re-dress, monitor closely.
- **Serious:** Increasing heat/pain, foul dark pus, red streaks under skin, systemic fever. *Action:* Re-open the wound, allow to drain, soak in hot water, and pack open with moist gauze.

## Evacuation Criteria
Evacuate rapidly (GO FAST) for:
- Uncontrolled bleeding or tourniquet application.
- Wounds opening a joint space/body cavity, or involving tendons/ligaments.
- Crushing injuries, impalements, or deep facial wounds.
- Spreading or serious infection.
- Animal bites or open fractures.
