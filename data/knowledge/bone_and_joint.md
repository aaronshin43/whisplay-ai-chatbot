# Bone, Joint, and Spinal Injuries

## General Assessment and Care
**Types:** Strain (muscle/tendon), Sprain (ligament), Fracture (broken bone), Dislocation (bone out of joint).
**Signs (DOTS):** Deformity, Open injuries, Tenderness, Swelling. Loss of circulation, sensation, or motion (CSM) beyond the injury is a serious complication.
**Management (RICE protocol):**
- **Rest:** Avoid painful movement.
- **Immobilize:** Stabilize the injured part.
- **Cold:** Apply cold pack (20 mins), avoiding direct contact with open fractures.
- **Elevate:** Raise above heart level unless it causes pain or a fracture is suspected.

## Splinting Guidelines
- Splint *only* if movement is needed and if it doesn't cause extra pain.
- Check CSM (perfusion/sensation) **before and after** splinting. If absent, loosen and reposition.
- If a limb has **no pulse**, gently attempt to realign (reduce) it to restore circulation before splinting. If pulse is present, do *not* force realignment.
- Splint above and below the fracture/injured joint. 
- Pad the splint well and fill hollows.
- **Open Fractures:** Control bleeding, irrigate heavily, cover with saline-soaked gauze. Splint, but leave a window to monitor the wound. Do not push protruding bone ends in if help is nearby (if >4-6 hrs away, apply gentle traction to pull bone ends under skin).

## Spinal and Pelvic Immobilization
- **Spinal Immobilization:** Required for unconscious trauma patients or conscious patients with neck pain/numbness. Keep the patient flat, stabilize the head in-line manually, use towel rolls/sandbags, and tape to secure. *Never tape the head to the bed.*
- **Log Roll Technique:** Used to move a spinal patient (e.g., if vomiting). Needs 3+ people. The person at the head commands the "1-2-3 roll" to keep the spine perfectly aligned.
- **Pregnant Patients (>20 weeks):** Immobilize, then pad under the right side of the board to tilt the patient onto her left side (prevents blood vessel compression).
- **Pelvic Binder:** For suspected pelvic fractures (pain, shock, hip bruising). Log roll the patient onto a folded sheet centered over the greater trochanters (hips). Cross and tie firmly at the front.

## Managing Specific Injuries
- **Shoulder Dislocation (Stimson Technique):** Person lies face down with the injured arm hanging. Tie a 5-10 lb weight to the wrist and wait 20-30 mins for gradual reduction.
- **Collarbone:** Secure with a sling-and-swathe. 

## Evacuation Criteria
Evacuate rapidly (GO FAST) for:
- Angulated/open fractures.
- Pelvis, hip, or thigh fractures.
- Multiple long-bone fractures.
- Loss of circulation/sensation (CSM) beyond the injury site.
