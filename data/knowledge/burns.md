# Burns Management

## Burn Assessment
**Depth Classification**
- **Superficial (First-Degree):** Red, painful, intact skin, no blisters. Quick capillary refill.
- **Partial-Thickness (Second-Degree):** Red/mottled, painful, blisters, wet. Temporary whitening on pressure.
- **Full-Thickness (Third-Degree):** White/black, painless (nerve endings destroyed), leathery/dry, no color change on pressure. Charred or pale skin.

**Extent (Total Body Surface Area - TBSA)**
- **Rule of Nines (Adults):** Head (9%), Arms (9% each), Front Chest (9%), Front Abdomen (9%), Upper Back (9%), Lower Back (9%), Thighs (9% each), Lower Legs (9% each), Genitals (1%). 
- **Rule of Palmar Surface:** The patient's palm and fingers (inner surface) = 1% TBSA. Do not include superficial burns in TBSA calculation for fluid resuscitation.
- **Airway Complications:** Suspect airway involvement if burns are to the face/neck, soot in mouth/nose, singed facial hair, or dry cough. Always manage cardiac and respiratory problems first.

## Care and Management
1. **Stop the burning process:** Remove heat source/smother flames. For chemical burns, brush off dry chemicals and flood with water. For electrical burns, ensure power is off first.
2. **Cooling:** Cool the burn with cold running water for at least 20 minutes (do **NOT** apply ice, as it worsens tissue damage). 
3. **Cleaning:** Use sterile technique and normal saline to clean. Carefully remove loose dead skin/broken blisters, but leave intact blisters unbroken.
4. **Dressing:** Apply a thin layer of wound gel and cover with a non-adherent dressing, gauze pad, or clean plastic wrap (if immediate transfer). Elevate extremities if possible.
5. **Pain Management:** Administer pain relief early and consistently. Re-dress daily if transferring is delayed.

## Fluid Resuscitation (Parkland Formula)
Initiate fluid resuscitation for partial/full-thickness burns ≥ 15% TBSA in adults (≥ 10% in children). Do not use the formula beyond 24 hours post-burn.
- **Formula (1st 24 hours):** `4 ml × Patient Weight (kg) × % TBSA`
- Administer the first half of the calculated volume within the **first 8 hours** (from the time of the burn incident).
- Administer the second half over the subsequent 16 hours.
- **Adults:** Normal Saline or Ringer's Lactate. **Children:** Ringer's Lactate/Saline + 5% Dextrose.

## Evacuation/Transfer Criteria
Evacuate rapidly or transfer to a specialized unit for:
- Trouble breathing (suspected airway burn).
- Burns >15% TBSA (or full-thickness >5% TBSA).
- Burns involving the head, face, neck, hands, feet, groin/genitals, or joints.
- Circumferential burns.
- Electrical, chemical, or explosion burns.
- Any partial or full-thickness burn in children < 5 or adults > 60.
