## Acute Coronary Syndrome (Heart Attack / ACS)

### Recognition

A heart attack (acute myocardial infarction) occurs when blood flow to part of the heart muscle is blocked. Recognize with SAMPLE history and ABCDE assessment.

**Classic symptoms:**
- Chest pain or pressure — crushing, squeezing, or tight sensation
- Pain radiating to left arm, jaw, neck, or back
- Shortness of breath
- Diaphoresis (profuse sweating)
- Nausea or vomiting
- Anxiety or feeling of impending doom
- Pale, cool, clammy skin

**Atypical presentations (especially in women, elderly, diabetics):**
- Epigastric (upper abdominal) pain or indigestion
- Fatigue or weakness alone
- No chest pain at all — "silent MI"

**OPQRST assessment for chest pain:**
- **Onset:** Sudden or gradual?
- **Provocation/Palliation:** Worse with exertion? Relieved by rest or nitrates?
- **Quality:** Crushing, stabbing, burning, pressure?
- **Radiation:** Does it spread to arm, jaw, back?
- **Severity:** 0–10 pain scale
- **Time:** How long has it lasted?

### Management

1. **Position:** Sit the patient upright or in position of comfort; reduce exertion.
2. **Oxygen:** Give supplemental O₂ if SpO₂ < 94% or signs of respiratory distress.
3. **Aspirin:** Give **300–325 mg aspirin** (crushed or chewed, not swallowed whole) if:
   - No known allergy to aspirin
   - No active GI bleeding
   - Patient can swallow
4. **Nitroglycerine:** If prescribed and available — 0.4 mg sublingual; may repeat every 5 min × 3.
   - Do NOT give if systolic BP < 90 mmHg or patient took phosphodiesterase inhibitor (sildenafil) in past 24 h.
5. **IV access:** Establish if trained and equipment available.
6. **Monitor:** Pulse oximetry, repeat vitals every 5 minutes.
7. **Activate EMS immediately** — do not delay transport for any intervention.
8. **Do NOT let the patient walk** — carry or use stretcher.

## Acute Heart Failure (Pulmonary Edema)

### Recognition

Heart failure occurs when the heart cannot pump adequately, causing fluid backup into the lungs.

**Signs and symptoms:**
- Severe difficulty in breathing (DIB), especially when lying flat (orthopnea)
- Patient prefers sitting upright or leaning forward
- Pink, frothy sputum (severe cases)
- Crackles (crepitations) in lung bases on auscultation
- Distended neck veins (JVD)
- Peripheral edema (swollen ankles/legs)
- Pale or cyanotic skin
- Anxiety and agitation
- History of hypertension or prior heart disease

### Management

1. **Position:** Sit upright — legs dangling (reduces venous return and preload).
2. **Oxygen:** High-flow O₂ via face mask; target SpO₂ ≥ 94%.
3. **Do NOT lay flat** — this worsens breathing severely.
4. **Activate EMS** — hospital care (diuretics, CPAP) required.
5. **Monitor** airway, breathing, and consciousness continuously.

## Pericardial Tamponade

### Recognition

Blood or fluid accumulates in the pericardial sac, compressing the heart and reducing cardiac output.

**Beck's Triad (classic signs):**
1. Hypotension (low blood pressure)
2. Distended neck veins (JVD)
3. Muffled heart sounds

**Other signs:**
- Rapid, weak pulse
- Narrowed pulse pressure
- Increasing respiratory distress
- Altered mental status
- Often after trauma, cardiac surgery, or pericarditis

### Management

1. **Activate EMS immediately** — pericardiocentesis required (hospital procedure).
2. **Oxygen:** High-flow O₂.
3. **IV fluids:** If trained — cautious fluid bolus to maintain preload while awaiting definitive care.
4. **Do NOT give diuretics** — this will worsen tamponade.
5. **Position:** Semi-recumbent or as tolerated.

## Cardiac Arrest — CPR Protocol

### When to Start CPR

Start immediately if the person is:
- **Unconscious** (no response to tapping and shouting)
- **Not breathing** or only gasping
- **No pulse** (or you are unsure)

**Every minute without CPR reduces survival by 7–10%.**

### Adult CPR Steps

1. **Scene safety** — ensure safe environment before approaching.
2. **Check responsiveness** — tap shoulders, shout "Are you okay?"
3. **Call EMS** — 911 / 999 / 112 immediately. Send someone for an AED.
4. **Open airway** — head-tilt chin-lift. Use jaw thrust if spinal injury suspected.
5. **Check breathing** — look, listen, feel no more than 10 seconds. Gasping = not breathing.
6. **Start chest compressions:**
   - Heel of hand on centre of chest (lower sternum)
   - Interlock hands, arms straight, shoulders over hands
   - Depth: **5–6 cm** (2–2.5 inches)
   - Rate: **100–120 per minute**
   - Allow full chest recoil — do not lean
7. **Give 2 rescue breaths** after every 30 compressions:
   - Head-tilt chin-lift; pinch nose; seal over mouth
   - Blow for 1 second; watch for chest rise
   - **30:2 ratio** (30 compressions : 2 breaths)
8. **AED:** Attach as soon as available — follow voice prompts. Resume CPR immediately after each shock.
9. **Continue** until EMS arrives, signs of life return, or rescuer exhaustion.

### Compression-Only CPR

Acceptable if:
- Rescuer is untrained in rescue breaths
- Rescuer is unwilling to give breaths
- Adult cardiac arrest (primary cardiac cause)

Compression rate: 100–120/min continuously without interruption.

### Key Numbers

| Parameter | Adult | Child (1 yr – puberty) |
|-----------|-------|------------------------|
| Compression depth | 5–6 cm | ~5 cm |
| Compression rate | 100–120/min | 100–120/min |
| Ratio | 30:2 | 30:2 |
| Rescue breath duration | 1 second | 1 second |

### Post-Resuscitation

- Place in recovery position if breathing resumes.
- Monitor airway and breathing continuously.
- Keep warm, prevent hypothermia.
- Do not give oral fluids until fully conscious.
- Transfer to hospital immediately for post-cardiac-arrest care.

## Differential: Chest Pain — NOT Cardiac

Always consider and rule out:
- **Pulmonary embolism (PE):** Sudden onset DIB, pleuritic chest pain (sharp, worse with breathing), tachycardia, leg swelling/DVT
- **Pneumothorax:** Sudden unilateral chest pain, decreased breath sounds on one side, tracheal deviation (tension)
- **Aortic dissection:** Tearing/ripping pain radiating to back, unequal BP in arms, new aortic regurgitation murmur
- **Oesophageal spasm:** Pain relieved by antacids or nitrates (may mimic ACS)
- **Musculoskeletal:** Reproducible with palpation; positional

## Emergency Signals — Activate EMS Immediately

- Chest pain lasting > 5 minutes
- Chest pain with shortness of breath, sweating, or vomiting
- Pulselessness or loss of consciousness
- Pulse > 150 or < 40 bpm
- SpO₂ < 90% despite O₂
- Systolic BP < 90 mmHg
