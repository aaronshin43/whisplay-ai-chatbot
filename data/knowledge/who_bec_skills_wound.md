# WHO BEC Skills General Wound Management

## Haemorrhage Control for General Wound Management
- Active wound bleeding condition: Stop bleeding using direct pressure, wound packing, or a tourniquet if required.

## Infection Prevention for General Wound Management
- Debris presence in wound condition: Clean the wound entirely of blood clots, dirt, dead or dying tissue, and foreign bodies.
- Periwound skin cleaning requirement: Clean the skin strictly around the wound thoroughly with soap and water or an antiseptic.
- Wound irrigation requirement: Thoroughly wash out the wound by flushing with at least 1 litre of clean water under high pressure.
- High-pressure stream creation: Use a syringe (with a 14g needle or IV catheter) or poke a small hole in a clean bottle and squeeze it firmly.
- Irrigation volume requirement: Use the entire 1 litre of clean water for flushing the wound.
- Unknown or outdated tetanus vaccination condition: Administer a tetanus vaccination immediately.

## Dressing Application for General Wound Management
- Perfusion and sensation assessment requirement: Check capillary refill, distal pulses, and sensation strictly beyond the wound before AND after dressing the wound.
- Standard dressing application step: Dress the wound completely with sterile gauze if available.
- Persistent bleeding condition: Apply a firm pressure dressing to the wound.

## Pain Management for General Wound Management
- Local anaesthetic availability condition: Administer local anaesthetic before cleaning the wound.
- Large laceration or fracture condition: Splint the affected area to manage pain and prevent further tissue damage.

## Transfer and Handover Protocols for Severe Wounds
- Open fracture wound condition: Handover the patient strictly to a surgical or orthopaedic unit.
- Amputation or vascular injury condition: Handover the patient strictly to a medical unit with operating theatre capability.
- Severe wound documentation requirement: Explicitly document the wound description, irrigation volume performed, dressings applied, tetanus vaccination status, and the exact time of tourniquet application if used.
