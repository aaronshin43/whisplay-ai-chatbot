## Wound Management: General Wound Management

### Haemorrhage Control
Stop bleeding as per bleeding control skills (direct pressure, wound packing, tourniquet if required).

### Preventing Infection
1. Clean wound of blood clots, dirt, dead or dying tissue, and foreign bodies.
2. Clean skin around the wound thoroughly with soap and water or antiseptic.
3. Thoroughly wash out wound by flushing with **at least 1 litre of clean water under pressure**.
   - To create high-pressure stream: use a syringe (with 14g needle or IV catheter) or poke a small hole in a clean bottle and squeeze it.
   - Be sure to use the entire litre.
4. If not vaccinated or not up to date, give tetanus vaccination.

### Dressing Wounds
1. Dress wound with sterile gauze if available.
2. Use a pressure dressing if the wound is still bleeding.
3. Check perfusion (capillary refill and/or distal pulses) and sensation beyond the wound **before AND after** dressing wounds.

### Pain Management
- Give local anaesthetic before cleaning the wound if staff and equipment are available.
- Splint large lacerations and fractures.

## Skill Assessment: General Wound Management

**Skill 1 — General wound management:**
- Haemorrhage control: stops bleeding per earlier taught skill
- Prevents infection: cleans wound of blood clots, dirt, foreign bodies; cleans skin with soap/water or antiseptic; flushes wound with ≥ 1 litre clean water; gives tetanus vaccination as needed
- Dresses wound: assesses pulses, capillary refill and sensation before and after dressing; applies sterile gauze; uses pressure dressing if still bleeding; checks perfusion after dressing
- Pain management: local anaesthetic if available; splints large lacerations and fractures
- Reassesses pulses, capillary refill, and sensation after dressing or splinting

## Transfer and Handover: Wounds

Patients with wounds requiring surgery (open fractures, deep wounds, amputations) need handover to:
- Surgical or orthopaedic unit (open fractures)
- Unit with operating theatre capability (amputation, vascular injury)

Document: wound description, irrigation performed, dressings applied, tetanus vaccination status, time of tourniquet if applied.
