# WHO BEC Skills Circulation IV Access and Fluid Resuscitation

## Adult IV Cannula Insertion for Shock Management
- Adult shock indication: Insert two large-bore cannulae (14 or 16 gauge) for any adult presenting with signs and symptoms of shock.
- Preparation requirement: Prepare the cannula, IV fluid, tourniquet, gloves, dressing, and alcohol swab.
- Vein engorgement step: Put on gloves and place an elastic band or tourniquet on the arm to engorge the veins.
- Kidney disease patient condition: Avoid inserting cannulae into arms with fistulae.
- Vein selection: Look and feel for a straight vein, and strictly avoid pulsating blood vessels.
- Shock condition with hard-to-find veins: Search the antecubital fossa (elbow bend) for access.
- Site cleaning requirement: Clean the skin with alcohol and do not insert the needle through broken or infected skin.
- Patient communication: Tell the patient what you are doing before proceeding.
- Insertion technique: Insert the needle flat and parallel to the skin, in line with the vein, and watch for flashback (blood in cannula).
- Arterial puncture complication (regular pumping blood): Remove the needle immediately and apply firm pressure for 5 to 10 minutes.
- Successful flashback condition: Advance the needle a few millimeters further, then slide the plastic cannula over the needle fully into the vein.
- Cannula securing step: Hold the cannula, apply pressure to the base, withdraw the needle, flush with normal saline, secure the site, and apply a dressing.
- Sharps disposal requirement: Dispose of the needle immediately into a sharps container.
- Daily maintenance: Check the IV site daily for signs of infection such as redness, pain, or swelling.

## Paediatric IV Cannula Insertion for Shock Management
- Paediatric vein site selection: Choose the hand (first choice), scalp veins, external jugular, antecubital, femoral, or ankle veins.
- Equipment preparation: Prepare a 21 or 23 gauge cannula.
- Vein visualization step: Keep the child's hand bent to obstruct venous return and make veins visible.
- Insertion and flushing step: Use the standard adult insertion technique, withdraw blood if needed for labs, remove the tourniquet, and flush with saline.
- Cannula securing requirement: Secure the site extremely well as children will attempt to remove it, but avoid wrapping tape completely around a limb.
- Joint protection step: Splint the joints near the IV site to prevent dislodgement.
- Daily maintenance: Check the IV site daily.

## Adult IV Fluid Administration for Shock Management
- Fluid selection: Attach normal saline or Lactated Ringer's to the IV cannula.
- Adult initial bolus administration: Give 1 litre of fluid over less than 30 minutes.
- Post-bolus assessment requirement: Assess response immediately after the bolus by checking capillary refill, mental status, urine output, pulse rate, and blood pressure.
- Complication monitoring: Assess continuously for signs of fluid overload.
- Persistent shock without fluid overload condition: Administer another 1 litre fluid bolus.
- Persistent shock after 2 litres condition: Reassess for ongoing external and internal blood loss or spinal injury, and call for an advanced provider.

## Paediatric IV Fluid Administration for Shock without Comorbidities
- Initial assessment step: Insert an IV cannula and weigh the child.
- Paediatric initial bolus administration: Administer normal saline or Lactated Ringer's at a volume of 10 to 20 ml/kg over 30 minutes.
- Post-bolus assessment with no improvement: Repeat the fluid bolus at a volume of 10 ml/kg over 30 minutes.
- Persistent shock condition: Call for help and plan handover to an advanced provider with blood transfusion capability.

## Paediatric IV Fluid Administration for Shock with Severe Malnutrition, Anaemia, or Overload
- Specialized fluid selection: Use Ringer's Lactate with 5% glucose, Half-strength Darrow's with 5% glucose, or 0.45% saline with 5% glucose if available.
- Fluid administration rate: Administer 10 to 15 ml/kg IV over 1 hour.
- Monitoring requirement: Reassess the patient every 5 to 10 minutes.
- Danger sign development (new/worsening breathing difficulty, respiratory rate increase > 5/min, or heart rate increase > 15 beats/min): Stop fluids immediately.

## IV Fluid Volume Adjustment for Special Medical Conditions

### IV Fluid Adjustment for Fluid Overload
- Fluid overload signs identification: Check for difficulty breathing with crackles on chest exam, lower body swelling (legs), inability to lie flat, or distended neck veins.
- Fluid overload adjustment action: Administer small boluses (250 to 500 ml in adults) at a slow rate, monitor closely, and stop fluid completely if signs worsen.
- Fluid overload critical warning: Do not give excess fluid as it can fill the lungs and cause severe difficulty breathing or death.

### IV Fluid Adjustment for Severe Anaemia
- Severe anaemia signs identification: Check for severe pallor to palms or mucous membranes, fast breathing, fast heart rate, confusion, restlessness, or signs of heart failure.
- Severe anaemia adjustment action: Administer fluids at a slow rate, monitor closely, stop if the condition worsens, and execute a rapid handover for blood transfusion.

### IV Fluid Adjustment for Severe Malnutrition
- Severe malnutrition signs identification: Check for visible wasting (bony appearance, skin appears too large for body) and bilateral foot oedema (dent remains after pressing the top of the foot).
- Severe malnutrition fluid selection: Prefer oral fluids if tolerated and add dextrose.
- Severe malnutrition IV adjustment action: Administer IV fluids at a extremely slow rate, monitor closely, and switch to oral fluids as soon as possible.
- High-risk adult malnutrition bolus protocol: Administer a 500 ml IV fluid bolus, reassess the patient, and give an additional 500 ml only if there are no signs of fluid overload.

## Uterine Massage Management for Postpartum Haemorrhage
- Abnormal postpartum bleeding cause: Recognize that failure of the uterus to contract after delivery causes most abnormal postpartum bleeding.
- Immediate response requirement: Arrange immediate transfer to a qualified obstetric provider, call for help, and start uterine massage immediately.
- Medication and fluid administration: Administer IM oxytocin immediately, initiate IV fluids and IV oxytocin, and empty the patient's bladder.
- Uterine massage technique: Place your hand on the woman's abdomen, cup the uterus through the abdominal wall, and apply strong pressure toward the patient's sacrum or lower back while massaging with a circular motion.
- Massage duration goal: Massage continuously until the uterus is extremely firm and feels like a 10 cm rock in the lower abdomen.
- Uterus relaxation condition: Resume massage immediately if the uterus relaxes after stopping.
- Placenta delivery step: Continue uterine massage and oxytocin administration, and collect and keep the placenta with the patient once it delivers from the hard uterus.
- Perineal or vaginal tear condition: Apply pressure with sterile gauze, put the patient's legs together, and continue oxytocin administration for at least 1 hour after bleeding stops.
- Persistent bleeding condition: Continue IV fluids with oxytocin, insert a second IV line, and transfer the patient immediately.
- Ongoing monitoring requirement: Continuously reassess for vaginal bleeding and perform frequent vital sign checks.
