# Bites, Stings, and Allergic Reactions

## Allergic Reactions & Anaphylaxis
**Non-Life-Threatening:** Hives, itching, nausea/GI issues. Remove the allergen (e.g., stinger). Assist in self-administering antihistamine if able.
**Anaphylaxis (Severe):** Life-threatening. Present if skin symptoms occur with trouble breathing or shock (pale, cool, sweaty, lightheaded), or if known allergen exposure causes difficulty breathing or shock.
- **Action:** Check for medical ID. Remove allergen. Assist with Epinephrine auto-injector (EpiPen®) straight into the outer thigh (hold 10s).
- **Evacuation:** Evacuate immediately (GO FAST) after any epinephrine use.

## Venomous Snakebites
**Signs:** Fang marks, localized pain, swelling. Nausea/tingling (moderate), shock/coma (severe), tissue necrosis.
**Care & Management:**
1. Keep the person calm and still. Do NOT let them walk.
2. Keep the bitten extremity at or slightly below heart level.
3. Remove rings, watches, and tight items immediately before swelling begins.
4. **Bandaging (Neurotoxic/Systemic):** Apply a broad pressure immobilization bandage upward from the bite. Keep it firm but DO NOT cut off distal pulses. Splint the limb.
5. **Bandaging (Cytotoxic/Local):** Do strictly NOT apply a pressure bandage. 
6. Gently wash the site. 
7. **Prohibited:** DO NOT cut the wound, apply ice, suck venom, or apply a tourniquet.

## Insect & Arthropod Bites
- **Tick Bites:** Remove immediately by grasping close to skin with tweezers and pulling slowly. Wash site. If flu-like symptoms occur within 2 weeks, seek medical evaluation.
- **Bee/Wasp Stings:** Scrape away stinger or use tweezers (don't squeeze venom sac). Wash site, apply cold pack. Monitor for anaphylaxis.
- **Spider (Black Widow/Brown Recluse) & Scorpion:** Wash, apply wound gel/bandage, apply cold pack. Use antivenin if available.

## Animal & Marine Bites/Stings
- **Animal Bites:** Control serious bleeding first. Wash minor wounds with soap/water and irrigate heavily. Leave serious wounds for a medical facility. Monitor for infection (rabies risk).
- **Jellyfish Sting:** Irrigate with vinegar for at least 30 seconds (or baking soda slurry). After stinging stops, immerse in hot water. *Portuguese Man-of-War:* Use ocean water, NOT vinegar.
- **Stingray:** Flush with water, soak in non-scalding hot water until pain resolves. Immobilize.

## Poisonous Plant Exposure
Wash affected skin immediately with soap and water (wear gloves). Apply baking soda paste or soothing lotion (e.g., Caladryl) to rash/sores. Wash exposed clothing thoroughly.

## Evacuation Criteria
- Evacuate rapidly (GO FAST) for: anaphylaxis/severe allergic reaction, severe reaction to insect/marine sting, serious animal bite (or suspected rabies), and venomous snakebites.
